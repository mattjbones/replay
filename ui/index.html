AI Trends Analysis
Forecast (next 3 weeks) iHolt-Winters double exponential smoothing on your trailing 12 weeks of activity. Dashed lines show predicted values; triangles mark forecast points. Captures both level and trend momentum.
Productivity Score iWeighted composite: PRs merged (x3) + issues completed (x2) + reviews (x1.5) + commits (x0.5). The dashed line is your 12-week baseline average. Trend is determined by linear regression slope.
Weekly Velocity iRaw weekly counts of PRs merged, issues completed, commits pushed, and reviews. Dashed lines show the linear regression trend for each series over the trailing 12 weeks.
Cycle Time (hours) iAverage time in hours from issue created to issue completed, grouped by week. Computed by joining matching source_id pairs in the activities table. Lower is better.
Work Day Types iK-means clustering (k=3) on daily activity vectors [commits, PRs, reviews, issues, messages]. Each day is assigned to the nearest cluster. Clusters are auto-named by their dominant activity dimension.
Tomorrow's Projects iNaive Bayes classification: P(project | day-of-week). Uses 12 weeks of historical data to estimate which projects you typically work on each day of the week, then predicts for tomorrow.
Work Patterns iActivity heatmap showing when you work. Each cell is a day-of-week x hour-of-day bucket counted over 12 weeks. Brighter = more activity. Helps identify your peak productivity hours.
Focus Distribution iStacked area chart of your top 6 projects by activity volume over time. Shows whether your attention is consolidating on fewer projects or fragmenting across many.
Burnout Indicators iTwo signals: off-hours work % (weekends + before 8am/after 8pm) on the left axis, and Slack message volume on the right. An increasing off-hours trend may indicate unsustainable pace.
